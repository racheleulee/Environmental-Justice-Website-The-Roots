Department of Energy (DOE) - Environmental Justice Documents and Publications

The United States Department of Energy seeks to ensure America's security and propserity by investing its resources into addressing energy, environment, and nuclear challenges throughout the nation. In each aspect of these challenges, disparities in environmental safety and human health remain huge among varous socioeconomic and racial groups. The link below contains a resource from the DOE that holds strategies, plans, progress reports, accomplishments, and information that helps address this problem and works towards achieving environmental justice.

CalEPA - California Environmental Protection Agency

CalEPA, or the California Environmental Protection Agency is a government organization that works to ensure that the state's environment is taken care of, working on restoration, protection, and enhancement projects to fulfill this mission. The organization does this in order to maintain public health, environmental sustainability, and economic vitality. Of its many programs, CalEPA has an environmental justice program that works to map the problem, collect data, pass policies, create solutions, and hopefully achieve environmental justice. The website linked below contains an enormous number of resources like GIS maps of pollution throughout California, policies enacted, grants, and publications that discuss environmental justice.

Los Angeles County of Public Health - Environmental Justice in Los Angeles County

After observing the resources that discuss environmental justice as a movement on larger scales, this resource does a great job at discussing this topic on a more local and small scale. It specifically discusses burdened communities in LA county and allows for an addressal of distinct problems in our local area. This can help create a better understanding of the real problem and the actionable steps that can personally be taken to help push this movement forward.
